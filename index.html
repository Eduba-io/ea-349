■ SECTION 02 / THE FRAME

Most vendors will sell you an LLM
for the entire workflow.

We do not. Here is the split we would hand you after a short diagnostic. Numbers are directional. The shape holds across every regulated hardware company we have worked with.

60% TRADITIONAL CODE & DATABASE

Structured storage. Templated generation. Version-controlled playbooks. Procurement-track dashboards. Postgres does this faster, cheaper, and with better guarantees than any LLM. If your team is copy-pasting the same certification package, that is not an AI problem.

30% RULE-BASED LOGIC

Certification-gate checklists. NATO-nation procurement rules. Helmet-integration compatibility matrices. Export-control and Canadian Controlled Goods flags. Deterministic logic. Auditable. The kind of rules that should never be vibes.

10% GENUINE AI PROBLEM

Reading a long technical document the first time. Drafting a first-pass certification or procurement response from your own prior artifacts. Translating SOF after-action feedback into structured product input. This is where a language model earns its keep.

Interpretable Context Methodology.

The certification and procurement workload is the textbook ICM problem. Stable identity and policy at L0 and L1. Procurement playbooks at L2. Per-nation and per-helmet-integration contracts at L3.
